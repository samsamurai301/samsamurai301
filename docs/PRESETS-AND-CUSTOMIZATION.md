# Presets and customization

Shiftframe is designed as an art-direction system rather than a collection of unrelated sections. Set the global visual language first, then refine individual sections.

## Choosing a preset

### Shiftframe

Editorial and publication-inspired. Use it when typography, campaign sequencing, product indexing, and story-led layouts should carry the brand.

### Chromalab

Energetic and graphic. Use it for limited drops, colorful product systems, youth-focused brands, experimental objects, and campaigns that need strong contrast.

### Pantryprint

Warm and tactile. Use it for food, beverage, wellness, ceramics, home goods, and brands that benefit from label, package, and material references.

### Quietform

Restrained and architectural. Use it for furniture, jewelry, premium technology, objects, and products where proportion and material should dominate.

Changing presets applies a coordinated starting state. Review every template after switching because merchant content, apps, and assigned resources remain store-specific.

## Recommended customization order

1. **Identity:** upload primary/inverse logos, set fallback wordmark and monogram, and add a favicon.
2. **Color schemes:** configure readable background, text, button, link, border, sale, and sold-out roles.
3. **Typography:** choose Shopify-hosted display/body fonts, scale, tracking, case, and label treatment.
4. **Art direction:** choose motif family, density, opacity, animation, media treatment, dividers, edges, and surface depth.
5. **Layout:** set page width, margins, section spacing, grid gaps, radii, and border thickness.
6. **Product cards:** choose Poster, Catalog, Split Label, or Minimal and configure information density.
7. **Motion:** select a motion level that supports the brand and test reduced-motion behavior.
8. **Templates:** assign launch, editorial collection, campaign, and brand-book templates where appropriate.

## Original graphic system

The theme includes six native SVG motif families: Orbit, Grid, Contour, Bloom, Rays, and Capsules. They inherit theme colors and can act as deliberate campaign graphics or as fallbacks when photography is unavailable.

Use motif density and motion sparingly. A consistent motif family across a campaign is usually stronger than using every option on one page.

## Product-card languages

- **Poster:** image-led, expressive, and suitable for launches or fashion.
- **Catalog:** information-forward and suitable for larger assortments.
- **Split Label:** editorial label treatment with stronger metadata hierarchy.
- **Minimal:** quiet presentation for premium objects and restrained collections.

## Content principles

- Replace all demonstration claims, quantities, dates, reviews, awards, and delivery statements with verifiable merchant information.
- Avoid baking important text into images.
- Keep headings concise and useful at mobile widths.
- Use one primary campaign action per section whenever possible.
- Remove sections that do not advance discovery, trust, understanding, or purchase.
